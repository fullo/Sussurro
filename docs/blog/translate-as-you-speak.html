Feature

Translate as you speak

Speak Italian, paste English. Speak English, paste German. The cleanup step can translate on the way out — locally, with no cloud translation service.

4 min read · Feature

This is one of the things a fully-local tool can do that cloud dictation often can't: turn your spoken language into written text in a different language, in the same keystroke. Because the same local model that cleans your text can also translate it, there's no extra service and nothing leaves your machine.

How to use it

  1. Open Settings → Cleanup.
  2. Set Translate to to your target language.
  3. Dictate in whatever language you like — the pasted text comes out translated and cleaned.

It even works with cleanup off

Translation isn't tied to a cleanup level. Set cleanup to None and a target language, and Sussurro does a translate-only pass — a straight rendering of what you said into the other language, without the LLM editing your style.

Per-app override

With per-app tone styles, each app can force its own output language — Slack in English, WhatsApp in Italian — overriding the global “Translate to”. Dictate once, land in the right language for wherever your cursor is.

Getting good results

Translation leans harder on the model than plain cleanup does, so a capable multilingual model helps. A 3B model handles common language pairs well; for tougher pairs or long passages, a larger model is noticeably better. See Choosing the right Ollama model.

Want the transcript in the original language too? Your history keeps the raw transcript alongside the translated output, and you can re-translate any entry to another language later.
