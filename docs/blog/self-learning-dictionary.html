The self-learning dictionary

Speech engines mangle names and jargon they've never seen. Correct one in your history and Sussurro learns it — no manual word list to maintain.

4 min read · Feature

Every dictation tool struggles with proper nouns — your colleagues' names, product names, technical terms. Sussurro's answer is to learn from the corrections you'd make anyway, Wispr-style, instead of asking you to curate a dictionary by hand.

How it learns

When you edit a history entry to fix it, Sussurro looks at the words you changed. Genuine spelling fixes — turning a mis-heard word into the correct one — are added to your personal dictionary automatically. Case-only tweaks are ignored, so “iphone” → “iPhone” won't clutter it.

How it helps next time

With the Whisper engine, your dictionary is fed to the recognizer as a hint, biasing it toward the spellings you've taught it. The more you use Sussurro, the better it gets at your vocabulary — names, brands, domain jargon.

You stay in control

The dictionary is just a list you can review and edit. Remove anything you didn't mean to add, or seed it manually with words you know will come up.

It's portable

Your learned words are part of the portable config — export them with your snippets and app styles and import on another machine, so a new install already knows your vocabulary.
