AI cleanup: the four levels

Raw speech is messy — fillers, false starts, no punctuation. Sussurro's cleanup fixes that with a local LLM, at whatever intensity you choose.

5 min read · Feature

After your words are transcribed, Sussurro can pass them through a local language model to tidy them up before they're pasted. You control how much it does with a single setting in Settings → Cleanup.

The four levels

None

No LLM at all — you get the raw transcript exactly as the speech engine heard it. Fastest, fully deterministic, and the right choice for a verbatim record, or when you're not running a model. (One exception: if you also set a target language, a translate-only pass still runs — cleanup off, translation on.)

Light

The gentle touch: remove filler words (“um”, “uh”, “like”), fix obvious false starts, add punctuation and capitalization. Your wording and tone are left alone. This is the everyday default — it makes speech read like writing without rewriting it.

Medium

Everything Light does, plus light editing for flow: smoothing clumsy phrasing and tightening run-on sentences, while keeping your meaning and voice.

High

The most assertive: clean, well-structured prose. Good for turning a rambling thought into a crisp paragraph — as long as you're comfortable with the model reshaping sentences.

Always safe

Cleanup never blocks you. If the model is offline, times out, or returns something unrelated to what you said, Sussurro falls back to the raw transcript automatically. You never lose a dictation to a flaky model.

Make it yours

Under Cleanup → Advanced you can override the built-in instructions for each level — write your own prompt for Light/Medium/High. Leave a field empty to use the default (shown as a placeholder). This is how you enforce house style, keep certain words, or ban em-dashes forever.

Tone on top

Cleanup is also where per-app tone styles and translation plug in — the same step can make your Slack casual and your email formal, or output a different language entirely.
