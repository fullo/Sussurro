Voice snippets

Some things you type over and over — an email signature, an address, a disclaimer. Say a short cue and Sussurro pastes the whole block, verbatim.

4 min read · Feature

Voice snippets are text expansion, triggered by voice. You define a cue phrase and the full text it stands for; when you say the cue exactly, Sussurro pastes the snippet instead of transcribing what you said.

Example

Define a snippet with the cue “firma email” and the text:

Best regards,
Francesco Fullone
DarumaHQ — https://darumahq.it

Now saying “firma email” drops your whole signature in, exactly as written — line breaks and all.

How matching works

  • Matching ignores case and punctuation, so you don't have to enunciate perfectly.
  • When a snippet matches, AI cleanup is skipped — the text is pasted verbatim, so your boilerplate is never “improved” by the model.
Deterministic by design

Snippets are exact text, not model output. That makes them perfect for anything that must be precise — legal disclaimers, addresses, code stubs, canned replies.

Set them up

Add snippets in the Personalization section: a cue and its text. They travel with your portable config, so your whole library moves between machines in one file.
