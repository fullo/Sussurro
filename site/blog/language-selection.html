Language selection & auto-detect

Telling the engine what language you're speaking makes it more accurate — especially on smaller models. Or let it figure the language out for you.

4 min read · Feature

Multilingual speech models can detect the language on the fly, but detection isn't free — it can misfire on short utterances or accented speech. If you know what you'll be speaking, telling Sussurro up front pays off in accuracy.

Fix it, or auto-detect

In Settings, pick your dictation language or leave it on auto-detect. A fixed language removes the guesswork, which matters most on smaller multilingual Whisper models, where auto-detection is the shakiest.

Engine differences

With Parakeet, language auto-detection across 25 European languages is built in and reliable, so you rarely need to set anything. With Whisper, a fixed language is the safer bet when accuracy counts.

Language ≠ output language

This setting is about the language you speak. If you want the text to come out in a different language, that's translation — a separate control in Cleanup. You can, for instance, set your dictation language to Italian and your output to English.

When in doubt: fix the language you speak most, and switch to auto only when you genuinely mix languages session to session.
