Choosing the right Ollama model

Sussurro's cleanup is a small job — removing fillers, fixing punctuation, matching tone. You don't need a giant model. Here's how to pick one that's fast, accurate enough, and fits your RAM.

5 min read · Setup guide

The model doesn't transcribe your speech — Whisper or Parakeet does that. The model only cleans up the transcript: strip “um” and “uh”, fix grammar and punctuation, and optionally translate or adjust tone. That's a light task, and a small instruct model handles it well while keeping latency low.

The short answer

  • Start with llama3.2:3b — a great balance of quality, speed and multilingual ability. It's the default recommendation.
  • Low on RAM or want it snappier? gemma2:2b — lighter and faster, still solid for Light/Medium cleanup.
  • Want better tone-matching and translation? A 7–8B model like qwen2.5:7b or llama3.1:8b — noticeably better instruction-following, at the cost of latency and memory.

Rough sizing

  • 2–3B (q4): ~2–3 GB RAM, sub-second responses. Ideal for per-sentence streaming cleanup.
  • 7–8B (q4): ~5–6 GB RAM, a beat slower. Better at nuanced tone rules and translation.
  • Bigger: diminishing returns for cleanup, rising latency. Rarely worth it here.

What actually matters

Use an instruct model

Pick an instruct/chat-tuned model (the default tags on Ollama are), not a base model. Cleanup is driven by a system prompt, and base models won't follow it.

Multilingual, if you dictate beyond English

If you speak Italian, German, Spanish and so on, choose a model that's genuinely multilingual — llama3.2, qwen2.5 and gemma2 all qualify. It matters most for translation and for keeping the output in your language.

Latency vs. streaming

With streaming typing and cleanup on, Sussurro cleans each sentence as you finish it — several quick calls rather than one big one. Small, fast models feel best here. If you only use batch cleanup (text pasted on release), you can afford a larger model.

The tiny-model gotcha

Very small models (2–3B) sometimes answer a short dictation instead of cleaning it (“is this thing on?” → “Yes, it's working!”). Sussurro guards against this: if the output doesn't derive from your words, it keeps the raw transcript. If you see it often, try a different or slightly larger model.

Try one in seconds

ollama pull llama3.2:3b
# then: Settings → Cleanup → Model (the list loads live)

Switching models is instant in Settings — the dropdown reads whatever you've pulled. Dictate a couple of sentences at each cleanup level and keep the one that reads best on your hardware.
