Whisper vs Parakeet: choosing your STT engine

Sussurro ships two speech-to-text engines. One is built for GPUs and every language on earth; the other is built to fly on a CPU. Here's how to choose.

6 min read · Concept

Speech-to-text (STT) is the step that turns your voice into raw text, before any AI cleanup. Sussurro lets you pick the engine in Settings → Engine, and the right choice depends mostly on one thing: do you have a usable GPU?

Whisper (whisper.cpp)

OpenAI's Whisper, running locally through whisper.cpp with GPU acceleration — Vulkan on Windows, Metal on macOS. (Linux ships CPU-only by default; a Vulkan build is available as an opt-in.)

  • Languages: ~99, with strong accuracy across most.
  • Sizes: from Base English (148 MB) up to Large v3 Turbo (574 MB, quantized) — trade size/speed for accuracy.
  • Spelling hints: it accepts an initial prompt, so your personal dictionary can bias tricky names and jargon.
  • Best when: you have a GPU, need a specific or non-European language, or want maximum accuracy.

Parakeet (NVIDIA Parakeet TDT v3)

NVIDIA's Parakeet model running via ONNX Runtime — a single, CPU-optimized engine.

  • One model, 456 MB (int8): no size to choose.
  • CPU-optimized: roughly 10× faster than Whisper on a CPU with no GPU involved.
  • Auto-detects 25 European languages — no language picker needed for them.
  • Best when: you don't have a GPU (or don't want to use it), you dictate in European languages, and you want the lowest latency on a laptop.

Side by side

  • Hardware: Whisper loves a GPU; Parakeet is happiest on CPU.
  • Language reach: Whisper ~99 languages; Parakeet 25 European, auto-detected.
  • Speed without a GPU: Parakeet wins clearly.
  • Peak accuracy with a GPU: Whisper Large v3 Turbo is hard to beat.
  • Model management: Whisper = pick a size and download; Parakeet = one fixed model.
Rule of thumb

Have a GPU, or need a language outside Europe? Whisper. On a laptop with no GPU, dictating in a European language? Parakeet — it'll feel instant.

You can switch anytime

The engine is a single setting. Try both on your own hardware with a paragraph of real dictation and see which feels right — accuracy and latency both depend on your machine. Whichever you pick, the transcript flows into the same cleanup, history and injection pipeline.
